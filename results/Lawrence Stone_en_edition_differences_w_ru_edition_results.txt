==Biography==
Stone was born on 4 December 1919 in [[Epsom]], [[Surrey]], England.<ref name="obit G" /> He was educated at [[Charterhouse School]], an all-boys [[Public school (United Kingdom)|public school]] (i.e., an [[Independent school (United Kingdom)|independent]] [[boarding school]]).<ref name="WWW" /> He studied for a time at the [[University of Paris (1150)|Sorbonne]] in Paris in 1938.<ref name="WWW" /> He then studied modern history at [[Christ Church, Oxford]] from 1938 to 1940.<ref name="WWW" /><ref name="obit G" /> His university studies were interrupted by service during the [[Second World War]] as a lieutenant in the [[Royal Naval Volunteer Reserve]].<ref name="obit NYT" /> He returned to Oxford after demobilisation in 1945, and after a further year of study, graduated with a [[First class honours|first-class]] [[Bachelor of Arts]] (BA) degree in 1946.<ref name="WWW" /><ref name="obit G" /> As per tradition,{{citation needed|date=June 2019}} his BA degree was promoted to a [[Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)|Master of Arts]] (MA Oxon) degree.<ref name="WWW" />
