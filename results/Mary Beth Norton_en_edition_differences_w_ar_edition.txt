==Biography==
Norton was born in [[Ann Arbor, Michigan]].<ref name="People"/> She received her [[Bachelor of Arts]] [[Academic degree|degree]] from the [[University of Michigan]] and her [[Master of Arts]] (1965) and [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] (1969) from [[Harvard University]],<ref name="People"/> under [[Bernard Bailyn]].

She is also noted as one of the authors of the two-volume ''A People & A Nation'', a popular American history [[textbook]], currently in its ninth edition.<ref>Articles written by Norton have been published in ''[[William and Mary Quarterly]]'', ''[[Signs (journal)|Signs]]'', and the ''[[American Historical Review]]''.</ref>

Norton has served on the [[National Council on the Humanities]], as president of the [[Berkshire Conference of Women Historians]], and as vice president for research of the [[American Historical Association]].<ref name="People"/> She also served as the general editor of the ''AHA Guide to Historical Literature'' in 1995.<ref name="People"/> Norton was elected a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 1999.<ref>{{cite web|title=Book of Members, 1780–2010: Chapter N|url=https://www.amacad.org/sites/default/files/academy/multimedia/pdfs/publications/bookofmembers/ChapterN.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|accessdate=December 20, 2019}}</ref> She was also elected Speaker of the third Cornell University Senate.
