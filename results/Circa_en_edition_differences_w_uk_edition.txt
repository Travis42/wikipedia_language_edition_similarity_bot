'''Circa''' ({{ety|la||around, about, roughly, approximately}}) – frequently abbreviated '''ca.''' or '''c.''' and less frequently '''circ.''' or '''cca.''' – signifies "approximately" in several European languages and as a [[loanword]] in English, usually in reference to a date.<ref>{{cite web |url = http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/circa |title=circa |publisher=[[Dictionary.com]] |access-date=16 July 2010}}</ref> ''Circa'' is widely used in historical writing when the dates of events are not accurately known. 

2. 
1732 – 1799: The beginning year is approximate; the end year is known precisely. 

3. 
1799: The beginning year is known precisely; the end year is approximate. 

4. 

When used in date ranges, ''circa'' is applied before each approximate date, while dates without ''circa'' immediately preceding them are generally assumed to be known with certainty.
