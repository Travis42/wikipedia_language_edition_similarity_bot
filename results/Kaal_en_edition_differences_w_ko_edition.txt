1. 

According to Monier-Williams, ''{{IAST|kāla}}'' 2 is from the verbal root ''{{IAST|kal}}''  "to calculate", while the root of ''{{IAST|kāla}}'' 1 is uncertain, though possibly the same.<ref>[http://webapps.uni-koeln.de/tamil/ Sanskrit and Tamil Dictionaries]</ref>

==As a deity==
[[File:Kala head, Candi Jabung, Paiton, Probolinggo, East Java, 2017-09-14 01.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Head of Kala carved on top of [[Jabung]] temple niche, East Java, Indonesia.]]

As applied to gods and goddesses in works such as the ''{{IAST|Devī}}'' ''{{IAST|Māhātmya}}'' and the ''Skanda'' ''{{IAST|Purāṇa}}'', ''{{IAST|kāla}}'' 1 and ''{{IAST|kāla}}'' 2 are not readily distinguishable. 

2. 

In the [[Chaitanya Bhagavata]], a [[Gaudiya Vaishnavism|Gaudiya Vaishnavist]] text and biography of [[Chaitanya Mahaprabhu]], it is said that the fire that emerges from the mouth of [[Balarama|Sankarshana]] at the End of Time is the ''Kālānala'', or "fire of Time".<ref>{{Cite book|title=Chaitanya-Bhagavata|last=Thakura|first=Vrndavana Dasa|publisher=|others=Translated by Sarvabhavana Dasa|year=|isbn=|location=|pages=203|quote=|via=}}</ref> One of the names of Sankarshana is ''k''ā''l''ā''gni'', also "fire of Time".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.stephen-knapp.com/thousand_names_of_lord_balarama.htm|title=A Thousand Names of Lord Balarama|last=|first=|date=|website=|publisher=|access-date=|note=}}</ref>

The [[Vishnu Purana]] also states that Time (kala) is one of the four primary forms of Vishnu, the others being matter ([[Pradhana]]), visible substance (vyakta), and Spirit ([[Purusha]]).<ref>{{Cite book|title=The Vishńu Puráńa: A System of Hindu Mythology and Tradition|last=Wilson|first=Horace H.|publisher=|year=1840|isbn=|location=|pages=ix|chapter=Preface|quote=|via=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Theory of Avatāra and Divinity of Chaitanya|last=Roy|first=Janmajit|publisher=Atlantic Publishers & Distributors|year=2002|isbn=|location=New Dehli|pages=66|chapter=Signs and Symptoms of Avatārahood|quote=|via=}}</ref>

===In the Bhagavad Gita===
At [[Bhagavad Gita]] 11.32, [[Krishna]] takes on the form of ''kāla'', the destroyer, announcing to Arjuna that all the warriors on both sides will be killed, apart from the Pandavas:

<blockquote>
'''कालो ऽस्मि''' लोकक्षयकृत् प्रवृद्धो लोकान् समाहर्तुम् इह प्रवृत्तः ।
</blockquote>
This verse means: "'''Time (kāla) I am''', the great destroyer of the worlds, and I have come here to destroy all people."<ref>See [https://www.bhagavad-gita.us/bhagavad-gita-11-32/ text and translation]</ref> This phrase is famous for being quoted by [[J.
