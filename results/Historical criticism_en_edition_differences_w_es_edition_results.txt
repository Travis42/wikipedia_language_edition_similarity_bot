The phrase "higher criticism" became popular in Europe from the mid-18th century to the early 20th century to describe the work of such scholars as [[Jean Astruc]] (1684-1766), [[Johann Salomo Semler]] (1725–91), [[Johann Gottfried Eichhorn]] (1752–1827), [[Ferdinand Christian Baur]] (1792–1860), and Wellhausen (1844–1918).<ref>The Columbia Encyclopedia, Sixth Edition, 2007</ref> In academic circles, it now is the body of work properly considered{{by whom?|date=July 2017}} "higher criticism", but the phrase is sometimes applied to earlier or later work using similar methods
